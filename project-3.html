The Literary Orientation Manual is a hybrid editorial format that merges the characteristics of a traditional manual with those of a regional guidebook. On one hand, it offers in-depth thematic insights into an author's works; on the other, it maps out physical itineraries through the locations where those stories unfold, allowing readers to explore the landscape through the lens of literature. The developed case study is dedicated to Bruno Brancher, a Milanese writer who made the city's working-class neighborhoods and industrial outskirts the beating heart of his narrative.
